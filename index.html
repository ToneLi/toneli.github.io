My Detail CV.

I am a researcher at Institute for Health Informatics and Division of Computational Health Sciences of University of Minnesota Twin Cities.

My primary research interests revolve around knowledge graph and natural language processing. Specifically, I am intrigued by topics such as information extraction, knowledge graph embedding, question answering over knowledge graphs, zero-shot learning, and more. A self-motivated and creative quick-learner with a strong passion for NLP and knowledge graph and proven skills in programming. My current research interests include:

  • Extracting structured knowledge with weak supersion: information extraction, knowledge-enpowered information extraction, zero shot learning, few shot learning.
  • Question answering: complex question answering over knowledge graph, multi-hop question answering, question answering with constraint. Question answering under zero shot or few shot learning.
  • Zero shot Link prediction.

Remember that discovering new problems and proposing effective models to solve them is a challenging process that requires sustained effort, thoughtful analysis, and proactive action. It’s not as straightforward as drinking cold water！！！！！！！
